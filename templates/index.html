Safe Synthesis

Prevention is better than Cure

This project helps chemists and researchers screen compounds for potential toxicity before synthesis or exposure. You can paste a chemical structure as a SMILES string and get a quick score from our model (currently mocked while training finishes).

What the model does

It takes your SMILES input, runs it through a machine learning predictor, and returns a toxicity score from 0 to 1—where higher values suggest greater predicted concern (placeholder scores until the real model is loaded).

What is SMILES?

SMILES is a text notation for molecules: atoms and bonds are written as a compact line, e.g. ethanol is CCO. It lets software share structures without drawing pictures.

Future Scope

  • Swap the mock API for a trained model with validated metrics and uncertainty estimates.
  • Add batch upload and structure coverage checks for SMILES parsing and edge cases.
  • Integrate interpretability: salient substructures or pathway-linked explanations for predictions.
  • User accounts, saved runs, and optional export of reports for lab notebooks.
  • Integration with scientific research databases (such as PubMed and chemical literature) to provide evidence-backed explanations for toxicity predictions using real-world studies.
  • An AI-driven system to suggest alternative compounds based on structural similarity—helping teams explore safer options without losing the chemistry they care about.
  • Advanced explainability through substructure-level analysis: highlighting functional groups and motifs that most strongly drive the model’s concern, tied to known toxicophores where possible.
